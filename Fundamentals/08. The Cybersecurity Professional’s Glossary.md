## Adversarial Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Using AI and machine learning systems in manipulated ways to make cyber attacks more effective.

### threat: 
Researchers fooled facial recognition using special glasses. AI identified the wrong person.

### Protect:
• Validate AI outputs
• Human review for critical decisions
• Harden training data
• Monitor model behavior

## Business Email Compromise (BEC)
A phishing attack where the attacker pretends to be a trusted person or company to steal money.

### threat: 
Facebook + Google lost $100M to fake vendor emails.

### Protect:
• MFA on email
• Verify payment requests by phone
• Dual approval for transfers
• DMARC/DKIM/SPF

## CISSP
Certified Information Systems Security Professional. A globally recognized cybersecurity certification issued by (ISC)².

## Computer Virus
Malicious code designed to disrupt computer operations and damage data or software.
### threat: 
ILOVEYOU virus caused $10B damage worldwide.

### Protect:
• Antivirus/EDR
• Patch systems
• Block unknown attachments

## Cryptographic Attack
An attack that breaks or weakens secure communication between sender and receiver.

### threat: 
MD5 collision allowed fake SSL certificates.

### Protect:
• Use TLS 1.3
• Strong encryption (AES-256)
• Disable weak crypto

## Hacker
A person who uses computers to gain access to systems, networks, or data.
### threat: 
Kevin Mitnick breached multiple corporations using social engineering.

### Protect:
• Least privilege
• Monitoring
• Awareness training

## Malware
Any software created to damage devices or networks.
### threat: 
WannaCry infected 230,000 systems globally.

### Protect:
• Endpoint protection
• Regular patching
• Backup data

## Password Attack
An attempt to break into password-protected systems or data.
### threat: 
LinkedIn breach exposed 117M passwords.

### Protect:
• MFA
• Strong passwords
• Account lockout
• Salt + hash

## Phishing
Using emails, messages, or websites to trick people into giving up sensitive information or installing malware.
### threat: 
Google Docs phishing took over Gmail accounts.

### Protect:
• Email filtering
• Awareness training
• Never click unknown links

## Physical Attack
A security incident that impacts both digital systems and physical environments.
### threat: 
Stuxnet entered via infected USB drives.

### Protect:
• Block USB ports
• Physical security
• Device control

## Physical Social Engineering
An attack where someone pretends to be staff, customers, or vendors to gain physical access.
### threat: 
Pen testers entered offices pretending to be IT staff.

### Protect:
• Badge checks
• Escort visitors
• Security awareness

## Social Engineering
Manipulating people to steal information, access, or valuables.
### threat: 
Target breach started with HVAC vendor deception.

### Protect:
• Zero Trust mindset
• Verify requests
• Training

## Social Media Phishing
Collecting personal details from social platforms before launching a targeted attack.
### threat: 
Twitter breach after attackers studied employees on LinkedIn.

### Protect:
• Limit public info
• Staff awareness
• MFA

## Spear Phishing
A focused phishing attack aimed at a specific person or group, appearing to come from a trusted source.
### threat: 
Clinton campaign email compromise.

### Protect:
• Executive training
• Email authentication
• MFA

## Supply-Chain Attack
Compromising trusted vendors or software to spread malware into target organizations.
### threat: 
SolarWinds infected 18,000 organizations.

### Protect:
• Vendor risk assessment
• Signed updates
• Zero Trust
• Monitor third parties

## USB Baiting
Leaving infected USB drives for employees to plug in, unknowingly infecting the network.

### threat: 
45% of employees plugged unknown USB drives in experiments.

### Protect:
• Disable USB
• Endpoint controls
• Policy enforcement

## Virus
Same as computer virus.

## Vishing
Using phone calls or voice messages to steal sensitive information.

### threat: 
Bank OTP scams stealing millions.

### Protect:
• Never share OTP
• Call-back verification
• Awareness training

## Watering Hole Attack
Compromising websites frequently visited by a target group to infect their systems.
### threat: 
Defense contractor websites compromised to infect visitors.

### Protect:
• DNS filtering
• Browser isolation
• Web security gateways
